Navigation thesis

How the product should feel on first contact

The public surface should read as a premium art marketplace with clear entry points for discovery, trust, and commercial action. The navigation must be simple enough for new buyers, but structured enough to signal that Arthentico-X has institutional depth.

Discover The broadest entry for the public catalogue, curated sets, and market browsing.
Artists The reputation and biography layer: portfolios, context, exhibitions, awards, and pricing progression.
Galleries A professional lane for institutions, represented artists, and batch-managed inventory.
Market Insights Editorial, research, valuation explainers, and premium-adjacent content.
Public architecture

What the open web layer must contain

The public product is not only a landing page. It is a structured commerce and trust surface: artwork detail, artist context, methodology, protections, and enough public price intelligence to make the product feel differentiated before subscription.

Marketing Home, about, valuation logic, seller acquisition pages, subscriptions, FAQ, and events.
Marketplace Search, artwork detail, artist and gallery profiles, collections, and watchlist previews.
Trust cues Seller identity, contracting party, protections, provenance, certificates, and shipping responsibility.
Conversion Buy, inquire, make offer, subscribe, or register, depending on user maturity.
Role surfaces

Who needs a private workspace

Buyer

Portfolio logic, saved intent, and post-purchase control.

  • Dashboard, holdings, watchlist, alerts
  • Orders, inquiries, relisting
  • Subscription and verification management

Artist

Inventory and reputation live together in the seller workspace.

  • My artworks, sales, inquiries, payouts
  • Documents, certificates, analytics
  • Profile settings and public highlights

Gallery / Auction

Inventory-heavy organization logic with team management.

  • Inventory, artists, bulk import, sold portfolio
  • Team members and documents
  • Organization settings and reporting

Admin

Moderation, trust, disputes, and platform control.

  • Approvals, moderation, compliance, valuation review
  • Orders, payouts, subscriptions, content
  • Audit log and import jobs
Motion through the product

The flows that matter commercially

Buyer discovers and buys Search, evaluate, verify shipping mode, transact, and see the work move into holdings.
Artist lists a work Build profile, upload documents and media, choose shipping mode, and publish if approved.
Organization uploads in bulk Import structured inventory with validation, deduplication, and admin review where needed.
Buyer relists Preserve provenance, refresh condition documents, and reopen liquidity from owned inventory.
Trust and platform logic

The flows that protect the marketplace

Admin review Inspect metadata, certificates, photos, and seller state before approving or deactivating.
Valuation workflow Normalize object data, refresh artist signals, generate confidence-banded estimates, and allow override.
Background revaluation Refresh market signals without mutating the seller-controlled live price.
Platform shipping Choose carrier, generate label, issue packaging instructions, and track shipment state.
UX operating rules

What makes the product credible instead of decorative

Artwork detail

The core conversion surface.

  • Image-first layout with price and valuation range nearby
  • Artist and artwork trend charts visible early
  • Evidence, provenance, seller protections, and shipping disclosed clearly

Portfolio

Ownership must feel actionable.

  • Holdings summary, performance, and liquidity signals
  • Relist access from the holding itself

Seller dashboards

Operational surfaces before lifestyle surfaces.

  • Inventory, inquiries, payouts, compliance state, and sales first
  • Charts as support, not decoration

Admin tooling

Queue-first and evidence-first.

  • Dense filters, side panels, side-by-side evidence review
  • Irreversible actions require notes and confirmation
Premium logic

What stays public vs paid

Public browsing remains the acquisition engine. Paywalling basic discovery would weaken marketplace growth. Premium should unlock interpretation, not visibility.

Keep public Artwork pages, artist pages, public price, biography, public highlights, and a basic valuation summary.
Gate behind subscription Comparable-sale detail, advanced analytics, alerts, and deeper artist-market research.
MVP discipline

What cannot be cut

Core conversion Artwork detail + evidence

The page where trust, valuation, and purchase intent come together.

Supply control Seller operations

Artist inventory management, gallery bulk import, and admin moderation are non-negotiable.

Retention logic Buyer portfolio

Owning, tracking, and relisting must exist early or resale logic stays theoretical.
